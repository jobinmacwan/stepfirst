Frequently Asked Questions

How do I set my daily step goal?

When you first open the app, you'll go through a quick setup where you can choose your daily step target. You can also change this later in the Profile/Settings tab.

Why does the app need HealthKit access?

StepFirst reads your step count from Apple Health to track your daily walking progress. We only read step data — we don't write or modify any of your health information.

Why does the app need Screen Time access?

StepFirst uses Apple's Screen Time API (Family Controls) to block and unblock apps based on your step progress. This is the same technology Apple uses for parental controls.

My steps aren't updating. What should I do?

Make sure you've granted HealthKit permissions. Go to iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → StepFirst and ensure "Steps" is enabled. Also check that your iPhone or Apple Watch is tracking steps correctly.

Can I unlock apps temporarily without walking?

StepFirst is designed to encourage walking before screen time. However, you can always adjust your step goal in settings if you find it too challenging.

When do blocked apps unlock?

Apps automatically unlock as soon as you reach your daily step goal. The app checks your progress in real-time.

Do blocked apps reset every day?

Yes! At midnight, your step count resets and selected apps become blocked again until you complete your new daily goal.

Is my data shared with anyone?

No. All your data stays on your device. We don't have servers that collect your information. Your health and app usage data is completely private.

How do I delete my data?

Simply uninstall the app to remove all data. You can also revoke permissions in iOS Settings for HealthKit and Screen Time.
